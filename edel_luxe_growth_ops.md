# Edel Luxe Growth Ops

## Live Store Diagnosis

Positioning today: visually premium, commercially diluted.

### What is already working
- The banner, palette, and product photography language read as elegant and feminine.
- Free shipping, returns accepted, and a 5.0 rating help trust.
- The shop has enough inventory depth to build topical authority around necklaces and gifting.

### What is suppressing growth
- The catalog speaks in too many aesthetics at once: quiet luxury, coastal, Y2K, boho, gothic, Halloween, streetwear, religious, novelty.
- Several titles waste the opening words on weak qualifiers instead of the highest-intent phrase.
- The first screen sells "Edel Luxe" visually, but the assortment beneath it does not yet behave like one brand.
- The shop-wide 25% sale trains buyers to see markdown as normal price, which weakens luxury perception.
- The live cherry listing is using a mismatched lead-image alt text that describes a pearl necklace, which is a quality-control failure and a discoverability risk.
- 66 listings with only 11 sales means the store needs sharper concentration, not more thematic sprawl.

## Portfolio Decision

- Pearl minimal necklaces
- Gold heart necklaces
- Herringbone, paperclip, box, figaro, and layered gold chains
- Sacred heart, locket, mother, holding-hands, and everyday gifting necklaces

- Cherry necklace

### Quarantine collection
Remove from homepage feature slots and stop using for brand-building:
- Halloween items
- Mummy, witch, pumpkin costume jewelry
- Brooches
- Red coffin necklace
- Kitty necklace
- Cuban streetwear chain
- Any novelty SKU that cannot be shot into the core Edel Luxe visual language

## Conversion Rules

### Title formula
`Primary buyer query + core item + material/style + use case`

Example:
`Minimal Pearl Necklace, Dainty Gold Choker, Quiet Luxury Jewelry, Gift for Her`

### Description formula
1. First sentence: item + style + emotional use case.
2. Second sentence: what makes it easy to wear or gift.
3. Bullets: material, dimensions, closure, packaging, care.
4. Final sentence: soft brand close.

### Gift language bank
- gift for her
- birthday gift
- bridesmaid jewelry
- anniversary gift
- mother daughter gift
- everyday luxury gift
- meaningful necklace
- jewelry gift box

## Thumbnail System

### First-image rule
- Product fills 65-75% of frame.
- Square-safe composition with calm negative space on all sides.
- No collage, no text overlays, no tiny product.
- Warm ivory, pale stone, or skin-tone background.
- Jewelry must be instantly legible at phone size.

### Listing image sequence
1. Hero close-up, product dominant
2. On-model neckline shot
3. Macro craftsmanship / clasp / pendant detail
4. Scale image in hand or on model
5. Layering combination
6. Gift-ready packaging
7. Styling scene with blouse / knit / silk
8. Dimension graphic
9. Materials / care
10. Brand mood image

### Visual variants to create
- White-space luxury flat lay
- Collarbone on-model crop
- Gift box with tissue and ribbon
- Two-piece layered styling
- Pinterest vertical crop, 2:3
- Seasonal version for gifting moments

### Ready-to-post examples
- The most useful jewelry is the piece that makes a plain outfit look intentional.
- Quiet luxury is often just good proportion, warm gold, and nothing extra.
- A necklace should survive both the white tee and the silk dress test.
- Gift jewelry works best when it feels personal before it feels expensive.
- Pearls do not need an occasion anymore. That is the whole point.

## Reddit Findings Applied

- Sellers repeatedly report that Pinterest can drive delayed conversion rather than immediate sales, so pins need clear product visuals and the landing page must convert once the buyer returns.
- Recent Etsy sellers discuss thumbnail crop instability, which supports keeping hero images square-safe with breathing room.
- Jewelry buyers still discuss skin sensitivity, daily wear, and durability concerns, so "hypoallergenic," "nickel-free," and care clarity should be visible when accurate.
- Fashion communities still value versatile, repeat-wear pieces, which supports leaning into "everyday jewelry" over novelty language.
